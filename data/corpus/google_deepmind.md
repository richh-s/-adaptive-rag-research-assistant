# Google DeepMind

Google DeepMind is the AI research division of Alphabet/Google, formed from the 2023 merger of
DeepMind (a London-based AI lab founded in 2010 by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa
Suleyman, acquired by Google in 2014) and Google Brain (Google's original internal deep learning
research team). Demis Hassabis leads the combined organization.

## Flagship product

Google DeepMind's flagship large language model line is Gemini, which powers Google's own products
including the Gemini app, Google Search's AI features, and Google Workspace integrations, and is
also available to developers via the Gemini API and Google AI Studio. DeepMind is also known for
landmark research systems built before the merger, including AlphaGo (the first program to defeat
a professional Go champion), AlphaFold (protein structure prediction), and reinforcement-learning
game-playing agents.

## Funding

As a division of Alphabet, Google DeepMind does not raise independent venture funding; its research
and compute budget comes from Alphabet's own resources, giving it access to some of the largest
compute infrastructure of any AI lab.

## Safety focus

Google DeepMind publishes a "Frontier Safety Framework" describing how it evaluates and mitigates
risks from highly capable models, and has a long-running internal AI safety and alignment research
group. It has also historically emphasized scientific applications of AI, such as AlphaFold's impact
on biology research, as evidence of AI's potential benefits.
