# OpenAI

OpenAI is an AI research and deployment company founded in 2015 in San Francisco by a group that
included Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, and Ilya Sutskever, among others. It was originally
founded as a non-profit with a mission to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all
of humanity, and later restructured to include a capped-profit subsidiary to raise the capital
needed for large-scale model training.

## Flagship product

OpenAI's flagship product line is the GPT family of large language models, accessed through the
OpenAI API and the consumer product ChatGPT. OpenAI has also released DALL-E for image generation,
Whisper for speech recognition, and Codex-derived coding tools. ChatGPT became one of the
fastest-growing consumer applications in history after its late-2022 launch.

## Funding

OpenAI's largest strategic partner and investor is Microsoft, which has invested tens of billions
of dollars and integrated OpenAI's models into products like Microsoft Copilot and Azure OpenAI
Service. OpenAI has also raised very large private funding rounds from a range of venture and
sovereign investors, at valuations placing it among the most valuable private companies globally.

## Safety focus

OpenAI has a public charter emphasizing broad distribution of benefits and avoiding uses of AI
that could harm humanity, and it maintains a "Preparedness" framework for evaluating frontier
model risks. The company has faced public debate over how it balances rapid commercial deployment
of new models against slower, safety-first release practices.
