# Meta AI

Meta AI is the AI research organization within Meta Platforms (formerly Facebook), tracing back to
the Facebook AI Research (FAIR) lab founded in 2013 under Yann LeCun. Meta has since expanded FAIR's
work into a broader Meta AI product and research effort spanning generative AI, computer vision, and
augmented/virtual reality applications.

## Flagship product

Meta's flagship large language model line is Llama. Unlike Anthropic's Claude, OpenAI's GPT, and
Google's Gemini, Meta has historically released Llama model weights publicly (under its own license
terms) rather than only via a hosted API, which has made Llama a popular base for open-weight
fine-tuning across the industry. Llama models are integrated into Meta AI's assistant across
Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger.

## Funding

Meta AI is funded entirely through Meta Platforms' own corporate revenue (primarily advertising) and
does not raise external funding as an independent entity, since it is a division of a public company
rather than a standalone startup.

## Safety focus

Meta has published responsible-use guidelines and model cards alongside Llama releases, and argues
that open-weight releases increase safety and innovation by enabling broader external research and
scrutiny of model behavior, a stance that contrasts with the more closed release approach favored by
some other labs.
